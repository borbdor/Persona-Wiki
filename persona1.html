Revelations: Persona (Persona 1)

Revelations: Persona, released in 1996 for the original PlayStation, is the very first entry in the Persona franchise. It introduced the core ideas that would later define the entire series: psychological themes, modern settings, and the concept of Personas— manifestations of the characters’ inner selves.

Story Overview

The story follows a group of high school students who, after playing the mysterious “Persona” ritual inside their classroom, gain the ability to invoke powerful beings from their psyche. Soon after, their town becomes overrun by demons, and an entire hospital is consumed by an alternate dimension. What begins as a school prank quickly turns into a fight for survival against supernatural forces.

Gameplay

Unlike later entries, Persona 1 features a more traditional JRPG structure with first-person dungeon crawling and a grid-based battle system.

  • Grid-based combat: Character positioning determines attack range and effectiveness.
  • Negotiation system: Players can talk to demons to obtain items, money, or Persona spell cards.
  • Elemental weaknesses: Early implementation of the series’ later famous combat system.
  • Multiple story routes: The game features branching paths based on player choices.

Legacy

While Persona 1 is much more traditional than its successors, it laid the foundation for the themes, character focus, and psychological depth that would later define the series. Its distinct mix of modern life, occult elements, and school setting was revolutionary for its time.
